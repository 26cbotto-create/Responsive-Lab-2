Sicily

Sicily's climate situation:

Sicily is suffering devastating consequences from climate change. A hot island in the Mediterranean, Sicily has been facing increased droughts because of increasing temperatures. Consequently, agriculture, a major economy of the island, is decreasing.

Sicily is risking desertification, a process in which land becomes a desert. Additionally, Sicily is at risk as sea levels continue to rise. The salination from sea level rise and desertification from temperature rise threatens to make living in Sicily impossible for many locals.

How to mitigate climate change:

  • Use green energy sources
  • Use more public transport and fly less
  • Eat less meat
  • Recycle more often and try to buy recyclable products

Many Sicilians are investing in alternate green energy sources to decrease carbon emissions. Sicily has very promising solar, wind, and water energy: it gets a lot of sun due to being near the equator, it gets a lot of wind due to being an island, and it gets very strong currents due to being next to a straight. Unfortunately, many government investments in these fields go to waste due to corruption and mafia presence.

“The climate crisis is both the easiest and the hardest issue we have ever faced. The easiest because we know what we must do. We must stop the emissions of greenhouse gases. The hardest because our current economics are still totally dependent on burning fossil fuels, and thereby destroying ecosystems in order to create everlasting economic growth.”
- Greta Thunberg
